# Search Skills – TryHackMe and Solent University Cybersecurity Coursework 

Platform: TryHackMe    
Skill Level: Beginner / Foundation  
Focus Area: Search Results Evaluation

## 🎯 Objective 
- Learn how to critically evaluate online information
- Identify reliable sources and avoid misinformation
- Develop research skills relevant to cybersecurity investigations

## 🧠 Core Concepts Learned   
- Evaluating information is a critical skill in cybersecurity
- Analysts must be able to distinguish between reliable data and misleading or false information

## Key Evaluation Criteria:
### Source
- Identify the author or organisation behind the information
- Prioritise trusted sources (security vendors, official documentation, academic research)

### Evidence and Reasoning
- Ensure claims are supported by facts, data, or technical explanation
- Avoid information based purely on opinion without proof

### Objectivity and Bias
- Check if the content is neutral or trying to promote a specific agenda
- Be cautious of exaggerated claims or one-sided arguments

### Corroboration and Consistency
- Verify information across multiple independent sources
- Reliable findings are usually confirmed by more than one source

💡 Strong research skills help analysts make accurate decisions and avoid false conclusions

## 🛠️ Practical Skills Developed
- Critical evaluation of online information
- Identifying trustworthy cybersecurity sources
- Avoiding misinformation during investigations

## 🧰 Tools Used 
- TryHackMe platform 
- Solent University Cybersecurity Coursework 
- Web browsers (research and validation)

## 🔐 Security Relevance
- Prevents reliance on inaccurate or misleading threat intelligence
- Supports better decision-making in SOC environments
- Improves the quality of incident investigations

## 📌 Lessons Learned 
⚠️ Not all information found online is reliable — always verify before trusting it 
⚠️ Effective search skills help identify outdated tools and detect misleading claims
