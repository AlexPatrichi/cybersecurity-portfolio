# Computer Fundamentals - TryHackMe and Solent University Cybersecurity Coursework 

Platform: TryHackMe  
Skill Level: Beginner / Foundation   
Focus Area: Computer Types & Usage Models 

## 🎯 Objective
- Understand different types of computers and their roles in modern systems
- Identify how computing devices are designed for specific tasks and environments
- Recognise trade-offs between performance, portability, and scalability

## 🧠 Core Concepts Learned 

## Computer Types 
- Computers come in different forms depending on their purpose, performance requirements, and environment of use

⚠️ No system is perfect — improving one aspect often reduces another

### Personal Computers 
- Includes desktops and laptops 
- Designed for individual use in homes or offices, offering a balance of performance and affordability
- Designed for everyday tasks such as browsing, programming, office work or gaming
- Laptops offer portable computing, providing an advantage through mobility

### Servers 
- Powerful systems designed to provide services, data, resources, or programs to other computers (clients) over a network. 
- Optimised for reliability, uptime, and scalability

### Mobile Devices
- Smartphones and tablets
- Focus on portability and energy efficiency
- Commonly used for communication, media consumption, and lightweight applications

### Embedded Systems
- Specialized computers built into larger devices
- Perform specific tasks with limited resources
- Examples: automobiles, washing machines, or smart TVs

### IoT Devices 
- Devices connected to a network that can collect, send, and receive data
- Example: sensors, smart doorbell, fitness tracker, thermostat

## Usage Models
- Different computer types are used in different ways depending on the environment and requirements

### Standalone Computing
- A single device operates independently
- Example: personal laptop used for local tasks

### Client–Server Model
- Clients request services from servers
- Widely used in web applications and enterprise systems
- Example: browser requesting a webpage from a web server

### Cloud-Based Computing
- Resources are delivered over the internet instead of local hardware
- Systems can scale dynamically based on demand
- Example: using online storage or web applications

## 🛠️ Practical Skills Developed
- Identified different computer types and their roles
- Understood how systems are used in real-world environments
- Connected theoretical concepts to practical examples

## 🔐 Security Relevance
- Understanding computer types and usage models is essential for cybersecurity
- Each system introduces a different attack surface depending on its role and exposure
- Servers are common targets for attacks due to exposed services
- Mobile, IoT, and embedded devices are often poorly secured, making them common entry points for attackers
- Cloud systems introduce shared responsibility and new risks

## 📌 Lessons Learned 
⚠️ These systems often work together in modern environments   
⚠️ Not all computers are designed the same — their purpose defines their architecture, performance, and security risks  
⚠️ Understanding where and how a system is used helps identify its potential vulnerabilities and attack vectors
