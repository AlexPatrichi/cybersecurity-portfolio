## 🧠 Core Concepts Learned
## ⚠️ Threats, Vulnerabilities & Risk

💡 These three concepts are closely related and form the foundation of risk analysis in cybersecurity.

👉 **Threat** = Something that can cause harm  
👉 **Vulnerability** = A weakness that can be exploited  
👉 **Risk** = The potential damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability  

### 🚨 Threat
- Any potential danger that can harm systems, data, or users  

**Examples:**
- Hackers  
- Malware  
- Phishing attacks  
- Natural events (fire, flood)  

### 🕳️ Vulnerability
- A weakness in a system that can be exploited by a threat  

**Examples:**
- Weak passwords  
- Unpatched software  
- Misconfigured systems  
- Lack of access control  

### 🔥 Risk
- The likelihood and impact of a threat exploiting a vulnerability  

💡 Risk depends on:
- How likely the threat is  
- How severe the impact would be  

**Example:**
- Weak password (vulnerability) + attacker (threat) → account compromise (risk)

## 🔗 Relationship Between Them

💡 A threat alone is not dangerous without a vulnerability.
-  Threat + Vulnerability = Risk

**Example:**
- Threat → Attacker  
- Vulnerability → Weak password  
- Result → Account takeover

## 📌 Lessons Learned
⚠️ Threats, vulnerabilities, and risks are interconnected  
⚠️ A vulnerability must exist for a threat to cause damage  
⚠️ Risk is determined by both likelihood and impact  
⚠️ Understanding these concepts is essential for effective cybersecurity decision-making
