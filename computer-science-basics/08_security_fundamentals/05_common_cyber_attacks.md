## 🧠 Core Concepts Learned
## Common Cyber Attacks
- Cyber attacks are attempts to exploit vulnerabilities in systems, networks, or users  
- Attackers aim to gain unauthorised access, disrupt services, or steal data  

💡 Most attacks follow a simple pattern:  
👉 Vulnerability → Exploit → Impact  

### Phishing
- A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information  

**How it works:**
- Fake emails or messages impersonate trusted sources  
- Victims are encouraged to click malicious links or provide credentials  

**Impact:**
- Stolen credentials  
- Account compromise  

**CIA Impact:** Confidentiality  

### Malware
- Malicious software designed to damage or gain control over systems  

**Types:**
- Virus  
- Worm  
- Trojan  

**Impact:**
- Data theft  
- System damage  
- Unauthorised access  

**CIA Impact:**
- Confidentiality  
- Integrity  
- Availability  

### Denial of Service (DoS / DDoS)
- Attacks that overload systems to make them unavailable  

**How it works:**
- Flooding a system with excessive traffic  

**Impact:**
- Service downtime  
- Disruption of operations  

**CIA Impact:**
- Availability  

### Brute Force Attack
- Attempts to guess passwords by trying many combinations  

**How it works:**
- Automated tools try multiple password combinations  

**Impact:**
- Account compromise  

**CIA Impact:** Confidentiality  

## Attack Chain (Connecting Concepts)
💡 Real-world attacks often follow this pattern:

- Vulnerability → Weak password  
- Attack → Brute force  
- Result → Account takeover  

👉 This shows how:
- Vulnerabilities enable attacks  
- Attacks create risk  
- Security controls can prevent them

- Essential knowledge for SOC analysts and security professionals  

## 📌 Lessons Learned

⚠️ Different attacks impact different parts of the CIA Triad  
⚠️ Understanding attack patterns helps improve defence strategies  
⚠️ Cybersecurity involves anticipating and preventing attacks, not just reacting to them
